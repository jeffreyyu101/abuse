Abuse is Bad.

Physical Abuse

Physical abuse is physical force or violence that results in bodily injury, pain, or impairment. (source)

This can include:

  • Hitting
  • Biting
  • Hair pulling
  • Grabbing
  • Pushing
  • Shoving
  • Tripping
  • Kicking
"1 in 14 children have been physically abused" (source)

Verbal Abuse

Verbal abuse (also known as reviling or "verbal bullying") is described as a negative defining statement told to the victim or about the victim, or by withholding any response, thereby defining the target as non-existent. (source)

This can include:

  • Name calling
  • Criticizing
  • Publicly humilitaing
  • Put downs
  • Embarassments
"62% of Tweens (age 11-14) who have been in a relationship say they know friends who have been verbally abused (called stupid, worthless, ugly, etc) by a boyfriend/girlfriend." (source)

Abuse of "Male Privilidge"

Male privilege is a set of social, economic, and political advantages or rights that are made available to men solely on the basis of their sex. (source)

This can include:

  • Making all the decisions
  • Going out with the "boys", but not allowing her that freedom
  • Walking out on an argument and leaving her
  • Doing all the telephoning and expecting her to be there
  • Embarassments
"Seventy-five to ninety percent of rapes are committed by male acquaintances: family members, co-workers, classmates, dates, boyfriends, husbands." (source)
